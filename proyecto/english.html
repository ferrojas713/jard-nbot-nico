Rat House

Echinocereus brandegeei (Rat House) It is an endemic species found in Baja California Sur, Mexico. The flower is a broad, bell-shaped flower that ranges from purple to light pink with a bright red interior.

Old Cactus

The Old Man Cactus is notable for its long, hairy white fibers that cover its columnar stems. These fibers give it a white or gray hair-like appearance. These fibers serve to protect it.

Biznaga

Ferocactus wislizenii, Commonly known as the water biznaga, it is a type of cactus characterized by its barrel-shaped form. It is one of the most efficient plants at capturing dew water.

Sweet Pitaya (Dragon Fruit).

The sweet pitaya, also known as dragon fruit, is the fruit of a cactus of the Hylocereus genus, highly prized for its flavor and nutritional value. It is native to Central America and Mexico, although today it is cultivated in many tropical and subtropical regions of the world, such as Asia, South America, and even some areas of the Mediterranean.

Cyrtocarpa edulis(Mountain Plum Tree)

The "mountain plum" refers to a wild tree scientifically known as Cyrtocarpa edulis. It is a shrub or tree growing up to 5 meters tall and grows in warm, dry, and semi-dry climates. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, yellowish-green, and 10 to 20 cm long. The flowers are small and red or pink. It is also known as the "wild plum" and is a symbol of identity for many Southern Californians.

White Copal (copalillo)

White copal, also known as "copal de penca," is extracted from the bark of the tree by making diagonal cuts to allow the resin to flow and be collected. It is an aromatic resin that has been used by pre-Hispanic cultures and remains popular in Mexico and Central America.

Green Stick (Parkinsonia aculeata)

The Green Stick (Parkinsonia aculeata) is a tree native to tropical America, known for its resistance to drought and high temperatures. It is a small tree that can reach 6 to 8 meters in height. Its trunk and branches are green, and it photosynthesizes through them, as well as through its leaves.

Cardon (Pachycereus pringlei)

A succulent, columnar, arborescent plant with ascending branches that can reach up to 19 m in height. It generally grows in clusters called "cardonales." The flower is yellowish-white with pink or purple lines; the fruit pulp can be red, pink, or white. Flowering generally occurs from mid-April to late June. An important characteristic is that this species has three sexes (males, females, and hermaphrodites), although the flowers themselves are morphologically unchanged. It is a key species in the ecosystem, as its flowers (nectar, pollen), fruits, and seeds constitute essential food for different faunal groups, as they were also for the ancient inhabitants.
